Gastronomy

Peruvian cuisine is one of the most varied in the world. Along the Peruvian coast, there are more than 2500 different types of plates registered. It has an unconditional ally in the great variety of climates, the particularity of geography, a mixture of cultures, and the adaptation of ancient civilizations to modern cuisine, allowing obtaining from nature the most exotic ingredients that give its gastronomy a unique character and own.

Ceviche

Ceviche is a typical dish of Peruvian cuisine, and it has been so successful that its flavors have spread to other Latin American countries. To prepare a typical Peruvian ceviche, it is straightforward. Boil corn with sweet potato meanwhile cutting the fish and onion into small pieces. Once cut, mix the fish and onions, and season with lemon, salt, and pepper. Let the ingredients rest so that the seasonings are absorbed and cut lettuce in layers. Once ready, place all the ingredients on a plate around the fish as complements.

Frozen Cake

The frozen cake is a popular dessert of Peruvian gastronomy, with its birth date in the 1960s. Starting with boiling water, and once hot, add the milk and gelatin. Wait for the mixture to be thoroughly diluted and place the mixture in a container in the refrigerator. Wait 30 minutes to start a new layer. Repeat the steps and remove the old box from the fridge. Add a unique mixture to the container and return to the refrigerator. After 4 hours, remove the dessert from the refrigerator, and it would be ready to serve.

Chicha Morada

Chicha Morada is a beverage that originated in the Andean regions of Peru but is consumed all around the country. The preparation is fast and straightforward. Boil the purple corn and cinnamon in water; the corn can be shelled or unshelled. Once the water turns to a deep purple color, let it sit until it cools. Add sugar and lemon to taste once the mixture is at a moderate temperature. Once all the ingredients are integrated, serve in glasses for tasting.
